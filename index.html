Improvement 1

Lecture less

Wait, what's wrong with lecture?

As a teaching tool: Can be OK if used sparingly

As an instructional model: Basically terrible

Students in lecture-based courses across 225 studies of STEM classes were 150% more likely to fail and performed half a SD lower on exams than students in courses based in active learning. (Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences)

In a study of 6000 students in intro physics, those in active learning based classes scored two SD's higher on a physics concept inventory than those in lecture based coures. (Hake)

Students in academic lectures registered brainwave patterns consistent with those of deep REM sleep. (Source)

Lecture also:

Disadvantages students from underrepresented groups and first-generation college students

Fails to produce results commensurate with time invested

Creates one-directional power relationship between prof and students

Does not scale up well to large and diverse teaching loads

Is boring unless you are gifted, which you aren't

Improvement 2

Focus on process (let content take care of itself)

What is the purpose of a university education?

How does a person learn new things?

What is the value of making mistakes in the learning process?

What do you reasonably expect to remember from your courses 10 years from now?

Process trumps content

How not to worry about content coverage

Downsize your courses

Small, frequent formative assessment

Small amount of compelling material >>> large amounts of boring material

Lots of coverage != good teaching

What focus on process does:

Plays to students' strengths (and yours!)

You feel better about what you're doing

Take on the role of "coach"

Less prep, more teaching
